What It Looks Like Today

Blue Button was developed by the Department of Veterans Affairs and gave Veterans and their families access to their health records. The first version was limited to text. For patients with many health conditions, the record became unwieldy because of the lack of presentation and hierachy.

Design Objectives

  1. Improve the visual layout and style of the information from the medical record
  2. Create a human-centered design that makes it easier for patient to manage their health
  3. Enable health professionals to more effectively understand and use patients' health information
  4. Help family members and friends care for their loved ones

Awards

Each submission will be considered for all four award categories listed below. A review panel will select winners based on defined criteria. An individual submission can win multiple awards.

$50,000

  • Overall Design - $16,000 (1st), $6,000 (2nd), and $4,000 (3rd)
  • Best Medication Section - $5,000 (1st), $3,000 (2nd), and $1,000 (3rd)
  • Best Problem History Section - $5,000 (1st), $2,000 (2nd), and $1,000 (3rd)
  • Best Lab Summaries - $5,000 (1st), $2,000 (2nd), and $1,000 (3rd)

The most innovative designs will be showcased in an online gallery and in a physical exhibit at the Annual ONC Meeting in Washington DC.

A panel of curators will select a final design (that may combine elements of numerous winning designs) that will be built and open-sourced on the code sharing community Github.
